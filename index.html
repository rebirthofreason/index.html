About the journal

Rebirth of Reason is an online, student-driven philosophy journal. Our aim is simple: to create a space where young thinkers can test serious ideas without dumbing them down for algorithms, trends, or school rubrics.

We are interested in argumentative essays, short theses, thought experiments, and conceptual ‚Äúmanifestos‚Äù. Your work can be speculative, but it must be intellectually honest and clearly argued.

Submission guidelines

These are starter rules. Edit them freely so they match how you actually want the journal to work.

  • Length: Aim for 1500‚Äì3000 words. Shorter pieces are fine if the idea is tight.
  • Format: PDF or Google Docs link. Use a readable font, 1.15‚Äì1.5 line spacing.
  • Audience: Assume an intelligent student who has basic familiarity with philosophy, but not necessarily with your specific topic.
  • Referencing: Any consistent citation style is fine (APA, MLA, Chicago, etc.). Just be coherent and give sources for key claims.
  • Originality: Your work must be your own. AI tools may help with grammar, but the thinking must be yours.
  • Topics: Philosophy of mind, ethics, metaphysics, political philosophy, logic, philosophy of science, or anything that seriously questions how we live and think.
